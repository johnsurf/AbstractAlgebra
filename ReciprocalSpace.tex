\section{Reciprocal Vector Space (3-dimensions)}
Take three non-coplanar vectors $\a, \b, \c$ which form a right-handed system of vectors and form the vector scalar product whose magnitude is the volume, $V$, of the parallelapiped with edges given by $\a, \b, \c$ 
\[ V = \a\cdot\b \times \c. \] The set of vectors $\a', \b', \c'$ which are reciprocal to $\a, \b, \c$  is given by

\begin{eqnarray*}
\a'&=& { \b \times\c\over \a\cdot\b \times \c} \\   
\b'&=& { \c \times\a\over \a\cdot\b \times \c} \\
\c'&=& { \a \times\b\over \a\cdot\b \times \c} \\
\end{eqnarray*}

The reciprocal basis $\a', \b', \c'$ satisfy the following identities
\begin{eqnarray*}
\a\cdot \a'&=& 1, \quad \b\cdot \a' = 0, \quad \c\cdot \a' =0 \\   
\b\cdot \b'&=& 1, \quad \a\cdot \b' = 0, \quad \c\cdot \b' =0  \\
\c\cdot \c'&=& 1,  \quad \a\cdot \c' = 0, \quad \b\cdot \c' =0 \\
\end{eqnarray*}
 
\begin{theorem}[Reciprocal Basis has Reciprocal Volume = 1/V]
\[ \a'\cdot\b' \times \c' = {1\over V}  \]
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
\begin{eqnarray*}
\a'&=& { \b \times\c\over V} \\   
\b'&=& { \c \times\a\over V} \\
\c'&=& { \a \times\b\over V} \\
\end{eqnarray*}
\begin{eqnarray*}
\a'\cdot\b' \times \c'  &=& {(\b\times \c)\cdot(\c\times\a)\times(\a\times \b)\over V^3} = {(\a\times \b)\cdot (\b\times \c)\times(\c\times\a) \over V^3} \\
                                                       &=& {(\a\cdot \b \times \c)^2\over V^3} = {V^2\over V^3} = {1\over V}\qed
\end{eqnarray*}
\end{proof}
